# BADMOUTH DIGITAL — Visual Asset Guide

Image, Video, Animation & Design Direction for Every Section

February 2026

## How to use this document

Every visual in this guide is mapped to a specific section of the website copy. Each entry includes the section it belongs to, the type of asset (image, video/gif, SVG, diagram, or 3D), what it should show, why it matters, and an AI image prompt you can feed directly into Nano Banana or any AI image tool.

**Design rules across everything:**

- Clean, modern, minimal. No clip art, no cartoons, no cheesy stock photos.
- Dark backgrounds preferred (#1A1A1A or near-black) with white/light text overlays.
- Accent color: #CC0000 (Badmouth red) used sparingly for highlights and key UI elements.
- Phone mockups should be realistic iPhone frames with actual message content — not wireframes.
- Dashboard/UI mockups should look like real software, not fake placeholder screens.
- Photography (if used) should feel candid and real — working contractors, not models in hard hats.
- Aesthetic vibe: Think SaaS company meets blue-collar grit. Software meets the job site.

## HOMEPAGE VISUALS

### SECTION 1: HERO

**Asset Type: Background Video / Looping Animation**

**What it shows:**
A slow, cinematic loop of a lead flowing through a system. Think: phone notification appears → gets logged in CRM → automated text fires → review gets requested. Abstract but recognizable. Not a literal screen recording — more of a stylized visualization of data moving through connected points.

**Why it matters:**
The hero needs to immediately communicate "system" without the visitor reading a word. The visual does the heavy lifting while the headline lands. Sets the tone for the entire site: this isn't a design agency, this is a systems company.

**Direction:**
- Dark background. Subtle motion. Not distracting from the headline.
- Think Apple product page meets a dark-mode dashboard.
- Nodes/connection points that light up as data flows through them.
- Subtle red (#CC0000) accent on active connection points.

**AI Prompt:**
> Abstract dark background animation showing interconnected glowing nodes on a near-black (#1A1A1A) background. Minimal, modern, sleek. Thin white connection lines between nodes that pulse with subtle red (#CC0000) light as data flows between them. Clean and technical, like a sophisticated network visualization. No text. No people. SaaS aesthetic meets dark industrial design. Cinematic, slow motion feel. 16:9 aspect ratio.

**Alternative: Static Hero Image**
> Aerial close-up photo of a contractor's work truck dashboard with a smartphone mounted showing a clean CRM dashboard with active lead notifications. Shallow depth of field. Natural morning light. The phone screen glows with a modern dark-mode interface. Real, not staged. No visible brand logos. Feels like a candid moment on the job site. Clean, editorial photography style.

### SECTION 3: REFRAME

**Asset Type: SVG Diagram / Illustration**

**What it shows:**
The "pipe full of holes" metaphor visualized. A clean, minimal pipe diagram. Water (leads) flowing in from the left. Holes along the pipe labeled: "Missed calls," "No follow-up," "No tracking," "No reviews." Water leaking out at each hole. The small amount that makes it through reaches a bucket labeled "Booked jobs."

**Why it matters:**
This is the single most important conceptual visual on the site. It makes the entire Badmouth value proposition visual and instantly understandable. A contractor looks at this and sees his business.

**AI Prompt:**
> Clean, minimal vector-style illustration of a horizontal pipe on a dark background. Water flows in from the left side labeled 'Leads.' The pipe has 4-5 small holes along the bottom with water dripping out. Each drip is labeled with small clean sans-serif text: 'Missed Calls,' 'No Follow-Up,' 'No Tracking,' 'No Reviews.' A very small stream reaches a container on the right labeled 'Booked Jobs.' The pipe is white/light gray on a near-black background. Minimal line art style. No gradients. Red (#CC0000) accent on the drip drops. Technical diagram aesthetic, not cartoon.

### SECTION 4: PATHWAY INTRODUCTION

**Asset Type: Layered Diagram / Animated Build-Up**

**What it shows:**
The three-layer system visualized as a stacked structure. Layer 1 (Foundation) is the base — solid, heavy, structural. Layer 2 (Visibility) sits on top — expanding reach. Layer 3 (Volume) on top — accelerating. Each layer is labeled with its components. The visual communicates that you can't add Layer 2 without Layer 1, and you can't add Layer 3 without Layer 2.

**Direction:**
- Could be an isometric 3D diagram or a clean flat illustration.
- The Foundation layer should feel heavy and solid. Concrete. Structural.
- Visibility layer should feel like growth — branching, expanding.
- Volume layer should feel like acceleration — arrows, momentum.
- Animated version: layers build up one at a time as you scroll.

**AI Prompt:**
> Clean isometric 3D diagram on a dark background showing three stacked layers. Bottom layer is solid and heavy, labeled 'Foundation' in clean white text — shows icons for website, CRM, automation, tracking. Middle layer sits on top, labeled 'Visibility' — shows organic growth lines branching outward. Top layer labeled 'Volume' — shows accelerating arrows pointing upward. Minimal color palette: dark background (#1A1A1A), white/gray structures, subtle red (#CC0000) accents on key icons. Modern SaaS infographic style. No gradients. No drop shadows. Technical and clean.

## TESTIMONIAL TREATMENT

Testimonials should NOT be in a basic quote block or carousel. Each testimonial gets:

- The quote in large, slightly off-white italic text on dark background
- A small identifier below: trade, team size, timeframe ("HVAC — 4 Techs — 90 Days")
- No headshots (unless real). No stock photos. No company logos.
- The metric that matters most pulled out as a large accent number next to the quote

Example: The quote sits left. To the right, a large "8 → 70" in red text with "Google Reviews" underneath. The number does the talking.
