**BADMOUTH DIGITAL**

Visual Asset Guide

Image, Video, Animation & Design Direction for Every Section

February 2026

**How to use this document**

Every visual in this guide is mapped to a specific section of the
website copy. Each entry includes the section it belongs to, the type of
asset (image, video/gif, SVG, diagram, or 3D), what it should show, why
it matters, and an AI image prompt you can feed directly into Nano
Banana or any AI image tool.

**Design rules across everything:**

• Clean, modern, minimal. No clip art, no cartoons, no cheesy stock
photos.

• Dark backgrounds preferred (#1A1A1A or near-black) with white/light
text overlays.

• Accent color: #CC0000 (Badmouth red) used sparingly for highlights and
key UI elements.

• Phone mockups should be realistic iPhone frames with actual message
content --- not wireframes.

• Dashboard/UI mockups should look like real software, not fake
placeholder screens.

• Photography (if used) should feel candid and real --- working
contractors, not models in hard hats.

• Aesthetic vibe: Think SaaS company meets blue-collar grit. Software
meets the job site.

**HOMEPAGE VISUALS**

**SECTION 1: HERO**

**Asset Type: Background Video / Looping Animation**

**What it shows:**

A slow, cinematic loop of a lead flowing through a system. Think: phone
notification appears → gets logged in CRM → automated text fires →
review gets requested. Abstract but recognizable. Not a literal screen
recording --- more of a stylized visualization of data moving through
connected points.

**Why it matters:**

The hero needs to immediately communicate "system" without the visitor
reading a word. The visual does the heavy lifting while the headline
lands. Sets the tone for the entire site: this isn't a design agency,
this is a systems company.

**Direction:**

• Dark background. Subtle motion. Not distracting from the headline.

• Think Apple product page meets a dark-mode dashboard.

• Nodes/connection points that light up as data flows through them.

• Subtle red (#CC0000) accent on active connection points.

**AI Prompt:**

> Abstract dark background animation showing interconnected glowing
> nodes on a near-black (#1A1A1A) background. Minimal, modern, sleek.
> Thin white connection lines between nodes that pulse with subtle red
> (#CC0000) light as data flows between them. Clean and technical, like
> a sophisticated network visualization. No text. No people. SaaS
> aesthetic meets dark industrial design. Cinematic, slow motion feel.
> 16:9 aspect ratio.

**Alternative: Static Hero Image**

> Aerial close-up photo of a contractor\'s work truck dashboard with a
> smartphone mounted showing a clean CRM dashboard with active lead
> notifications. Shallow depth of field. Natural morning light. The
> phone screen glows with a modern dark-mode interface. Real, not
> staged. No visible brand logos. Feels like a candid moment on the job
> site. Clean, editorial photography style.

• Visibility layer should feel like growth --- branching, expanding.

• Volume layer should feel like acceleration --- arrows, momentum.

• Animated version: layers build up one at a time as you scroll.

> Clean isometric 3D diagram on a dark background showing three stacked

**TESTIMONIAL TREATMENT**

**Asset Type: Design Direction**

Testimonials should NOT be in a basic quote block or carousel. Each
testimonial gets:

• The quote in large, slightly off-white italic text on dark background

• A small identifier below: trade, team size, timeframe ("HVAC --- 4
Techs --- 90 Days")

• No headshots (unless real). No stock photos. No company logos.

• The metric that matters most pulled out as a large accent number next
to the quote

Example: The quote sits left. To the right, a large "8 → 70" in red text
with "Google Reviews" underneath. The number does the talking.
