04 / Belief Stack

The Four Beliefs

A buyer must hold all four beliefs before they are ready to commit. Missing even one kills the sale. Marketing fails when it pitches at a stage the buyer hasn't reached yet.

Layer 1 — Situation Belief
[e.g. I've been dealing with this for a while but I always thought it was just part of the deal. I didn't realize how much it was actually costing me.]
Marketing Rule [e.g. Don't pitch the solution here. Name the situation. Validate the pain. Make them feel seen before you offer anything. The goal is recognition, not persuasion.]
Layer 2 — Cause Belief
[e.g. I kept trying to fix it on my own but I didn't realize the real issue was the system, not the effort. No wonder nothing was sticking.]
Marketing Rule [e.g. Teach, don't sell. Content that explains the root cause builds the most trust and pre-sells your approach before a pitch ever lands. Reframe the problem so your solution is the logical next step.]
Layer 3 — Fix Belief
[e.g. I was skeptical, but when I saw how someone like me got a real result from this approach, I thought — maybe this could actually work for me too.]
Marketing Rule [e.g. Use case studies from people who match their profile exactly. Abstracted results don't land — specificity does. Show the mechanism, not just the outcome. Prove the category before proving the brand.]
Layer 4 — Provider Trust
[e.g. I looked at a few options but [Brand Name] felt like they actually understood my specific situation — not just the general problem.]
Marketing Rule [e.g. Proof, specificity, alignment. Show you understand them specifically — not just the category. Testimonials, credentials, and any friction-reduction (guarantee, trial, free call) live here. Remove risk from the decision.]

07 / Content & SEO Strategy

Content That Converts

Content mapped to the Buyer Belief Ladder. Each stage requires different hooks, different proof, and different calls to action.

Belief Stage Target Buyer Goal Hook Ideas
Stage 1

Unaware		 Buyers who don't yet see a problem worth solving — scrolling, not searching Pattern interrupt. Name a situation they recognize without framing it as a problem.
  • [e.g. "Most [ICP] don't realize [surprising fact about their situation]"]
  • [e.g. "If you've ever [common experience], this is why it keeps happening"]
  • [e.g. "Nobody talks about [uncomfortable truth in their world]"]
Stage 2

Problem Recognition		 Buyers who know something's wrong but haven't named it — frustrated, searching Validate the pain. Name it precisely. Make them feel seen before offering anything.
  • [e.g. "You're not imagining it — [problem] is real and here's why it matters"]
  • [e.g. "The hidden cost of ignoring [problem] (most people find out too late)"]
  • [e.g. "Why [common workaround] doesn't actually fix the problem"]
Stage 3

Cause Awareness		 Buyers who understand the problem but not why past attempts failed — skeptical Reframe the problem so your solution makes logical sense. Teach before you sell.
  • [e.g. "The real reason [common solution] doesn't work for [ICP]"]
  • [e.g. "3 things [ICP] keep doing that make [problem] worse — and what to do instead"]
  • [e.g. "I tried everything too. Here's what I missed."]
Stage 4

Solution Awareness		 Buyers open to a solution, evaluating whether it works for them — comparing Show the mechanism working for someone like them. Proof over promise.
  • [e.g. "How [client type] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]"]
  • [e.g. "The exact process we used to get [specific result] for [type of person]"]
  • [e.g. "[Before/after story] — and the one thing that actually made the difference"]
Stage 5

Provider Decision		 Buyers ready to act, choosing between options — evaluating trust and fit Remove risk, establish credibility, make the decision easy. Differentiate.
  • [e.g. "Why [ICP] choose [Brand Name] over [common alternative]"]
  • [e.g. "What to ask before you [hire / buy / sign up for] any [category]"]
  • [e.g. "[Guarantee / risk-removal statement] — what we promise and what happens if we miss"]
